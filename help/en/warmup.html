Email Warmup

Email warmup gradually increases your sending volume over 4–6 weeks, building sender reputation and ensuring your emails reach the primary inbox — not spam.

How Warmup Works

New email addresses have no sending history. Email providers are suspicious of sudden high-volume sending from unknown addresses. Warmup solves this by starting with a small daily volume and increasing gradually — signalling to Gmail and Outlook that this is a legitimate, trusted sender.

Warmup Schedule

Week 1: 5 emails/day
Week 2: 10 emails/day
Week 3: 20 emails/day
Week 4: 30 emails/day
Week 5: 40 emails/day
Week 6: 50 emails/day (ready for campaigns)

Domain Health Check First

Before starting warmup, run the Domain Health Dashboard (Killer Feature #1) on your sending domain. All five checks must pass (SPF, DKIM, DMARC, MX, Blacklist) before starting warmup. A domain with missing DMARC cannot be warmed up effectively.

Go to Email Accounts → Domain Health and enter your sending domain. Fix any failing checks before proceeding.

Warmup Metrics

The Warmup dashboard shows daily sent/received counts and a reputation score (0–100). Keep reputation above 80 before starting cold outreach campaigns. If reputation drops below 70, pause your campaigns and let the warmup continue for another week.

Multiple Inboxes

For high-volume outreach (100+ emails/day), warm up multiple inboxes and rotate between them in your campaigns. Go to Email Accounts to add multiple senders. Each account has its own warmup progress tracker.

Tips

  • Use a custom domain, never @gmail.com for cold outreach
  • Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC before day 1 of warmup
  • Never send more than 50 cold emails/day from a single inbox
  • Check Domain Health weekly during warmup
  • If you get spam complaints, pause immediately and investigate
