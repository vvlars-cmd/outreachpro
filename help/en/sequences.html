Email Sequences

Sequences are multi-step automated follow-up flows. Each step is an email (or LinkedIn message) sent automatically after a delay — with the entire sequence stopping the moment a lead replies.

Creating a Sequence

  1. Go to Sequences and select a campaign
  2. Click Add Step for each email in the sequence
  3. Set the delay (days after previous step): Step 1 = Day 0, Step 2 = Day 3, Step 3 = Day 7
  4. Write subject and body for each step using {{tokens}}
  5. Optionally add a LinkedIn Step between email steps

Recommended 5-Step Sequence

Step 1 (Day 0)  — Email: personalised opening + product pitch
Step 2 (Day 3)  — LinkedIn: connection request (personalised note)
Step 3 (Day 5)  — Email: follow-up with case study or social proof
Step 4 (Day 14) — LinkedIn: follow-up message after connecting
Step 5 (Day 21) — Email: breakup email ("Last one, I promise...")

This 5-step multichannel sequence generates 3–4× more replies than email-only outreach.

Stop on Reply

When enabled (recommended), the sequence stops automatically for any lead who replies — regardless of which step they're on. This prevents embarrassing "I already replied to this!" situations.

LinkedIn Steps (Killer Feature #7)

Any step can be a LinkedIn action instead of an email. Claude generates the connection request or message text based on the lead's profile. These appear as tasks in your sequence — OutreachPro notifies you to send them manually (LinkedIn's automation policies prevent direct API sending).

Sequence Best Practices

  • Each step should reference the previous one: "Following up on my email from Tuesday..."
  • Step 2 should be shorter than Step 1 — busy people prefer brevity on follow-ups
  • The "breakup email" (last step) often has the highest reply rate — something like "I'll stop bothering you after this" creates urgency
  • Never send more than 5 steps — more than that damages sender reputation
  • Space steps at least 3 days apart — daily emails feel like spam
