Lead Prioritisation

Every lead in your database gets a priority score from 0–100 and is bucketed into Hot, Warm, or Cold. The score combines: base lead quality score (from scraping), buying signals, email verification status, email engagement history, and Memory CRM follow-up flags. Sorted highest first — work the hot leads, skip the cold ones. Salesforce's "Einstein Lead Scoring" charges $175+/user/month for this.

Priority Score Factors

  • Base score — from lead scraper: rating, reviews, website, email, phone (0–100)
  • Buying signals (+15–30pts) — open = +15, click = +30, reply = +45
  • Email verified (+10pts) — MX records confirmed, not disposable
  • High-risk email (−20pts) — disposable or no MX = penalised heavily
  • Has replied (+30pts) — biggest boost: already engaged
  • Email opened (+10pts) — showed interest
  • Memory CRM follow-up queued — flagged in the list (no score change)

Tier Definitions

  • Hot (70–100) — reach out today. These are your best-converting prospects.
  • Warm (40–69) — solid leads, worth outreach. Personalise your approach.
  • Cold (0–39) — low priority. Either improve the data (get a better email) or skip.

Daily Workflow

  1. Go to Lead Finder → Prioritised View
  2. Filter to Hot leads — start your day here
  3. Cross-reference with Daily Task Queue — any hot leads with overdue follow-ups?
  4. Run Account Research on your top 5 before calling
  5. Enrol Warm leads in sequences; Cold leads in ICP-triggered bulk scraping batches

API

GET /api/leads/prioritised
→ { total, hot, warm, cold,
    leads: [{ ...lead, priorityScore, tier: 'hot|warm|cold', factors[] }] }
