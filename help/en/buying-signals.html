Buying Signal Triggers

Buying signal tracking fires the moment a lead engages with your emails. An open = 30 points. A click = 60 points. A reply = 100 points. Leads scoring 60+ are automatically flagged as hot and surface at the top of your follow-up queue.

How Tracking Works

OutreachPro embeds an invisible 1×1 pixel image in every email. When the lead opens the email, their email client loads the pixel and OutreachPro records an "open" event instantly. No external service required — it's a single HTTP request to your own server.

Embedding the Tracking Pixel

Add this to any email HTML (OutreachPro adds it automatically when sending via campaigns):

<img src="https://yourserver.com/t/{{leadId}}/{{campaignId}}"
     width="1" height="1" style="display:none"/>

Signal Scores

  • Open (30 pts) — lead saw your subject line and opened. Warm signal.
  • Click (60 pts) — lead clicked a link in your email. Hot signal. Follow up within 2 hours.
  • Reply (100 pts) — lead replied. Convert immediately. Lead auto-moves to "Replied" in CRM.

Hot Lead Queue

Go to Analytics → Buying Signals → Hot Leads to see all leads with signal score ≥ 60. These are sorted by recency — the most recently active leads are at the top. The sweet spot for follow-up is within 2 hours of a click event.

Why This Converts Better

The 2026 HubSpot State of Sales report confirms that signal-triggered outreach converts at 1.7× the rate of scheduled sequences. When a lead clicks your email, they're thinking about you right now. A follow-up call or personalised reply in that window feels responsive, not intrusive.

Combining with Smart Sequences

Use buying signals to trigger Smart Sequence branches: If lead clicks link → immediately skip to Step 3 (demo offer) instead of waiting the standard 7-day timer. This cuts your time-to-reply from days to hours for the hottest leads.

API

POST /api/signals/track           → record event {leadId, campaignId, event}
GET  /api/signals/hot             → leads scoring 60+ not yet actioned
POST /api/signals/:id/action      → mark signal as actioned
GET  /t/:leadId/:campaignId       → tracking pixel (returns 1x1 GIF)
