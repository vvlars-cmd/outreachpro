CRM Pipeline

The CRM Kanban board tracks every lead from first contact to closed deal. Drag cards between stages to update deal status. Revenue updates automatically as deals move to Won.

Pipeline Stages

  • Prospect — lead identified, not yet contacted
  • Contacted — first email or LinkedIn message sent
  • Replied — lead has responded (positive or requesting info)
  • Meeting — call or demo scheduled
  • Proposal — quote or proposal sent
  • Won — deal closed ✓ (revenue counted)
  • Lost — not moving forward

Memory CRM (Killer Feature #5)

When a lead says "call me back in Q4" or "ping me after the holidays", Memory CRM captures this automatically. When a reply comes into your Unibox containing a timing signal, click Add to Memory. Claude extracts the follow-up date and stores a reminder.

Go to CRM → Memory to see all upcoming follow-ups sorted by due date. Click Action to mark complete and trigger a personalised re-engagement email.

Examples Claude understands:

  • "Call me in Q4" → follow-up queued for Oct 1
  • "We're busy until after Christmas" → queued for Jan 2
  • "Try me again in 3 months" → queued 90 days from today
  • "Budget approved in March" → queued for Mar 1

Deal Value

Set a deal value when creating a CRM card. This feeds the Pipeline Value KPI on the Analytics dashboard. When a deal moves to Won, the value moves from Pipeline to Won Revenue — giving you a real-time view of your outreach ROI.

Intent Signals in CRM

Leads flagged by the Intent Signal Detector (rapid review growth, rating improvement) are highlighted in the CRM with a 🔥 signal badge. These are the hottest leads — prioritise outreach to them first.

White-Label Workspaces

Each client workspace has its own isolated CRM pipeline (Killer Feature #8). Switch workspaces using the workspace selector in the top bar. The master view aggregates deal values across all workspaces.
