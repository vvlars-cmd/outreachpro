Email Verification

Before OutreachPro sends a single email, it checks whether the address is actually deliverable. Invalid emails cause bounces. High bounce rates destroy sender reputation. This feature catches bad addresses before they do damage — saving you from the most preventable deliverability problem.

What Gets Checked

  • Format validation — does the address follow valid email syntax?
  • Disposable domain detection — mailinator.com, tempmail.com, guerrillamail.com, and 17 others are automatically flagged as invalid
  • Role-based detection — info@, support@, admin@, noreply@ addresses are flagged as low-priority. These route to inboxes monitored by multiple people and rarely generate replies
  • MX record check — does the email's domain actually have mail servers? This catches domains that exist as websites but can't receive email

Risk Scores

  • Low risk (score 85+) — MX records found, not disposable, not role-based. Safe to send.
  • Medium risk (score 40) — Role-based address. May send but expect lower reply rates. Consider finding the owner's direct email first.
  • High risk (score 0–5) — Disposable domain, invalid format, or domain has no mail servers. Do not send.

Verifying Your Lead List

  1. Go to Lead Finder → Verify Emails
  2. Click Verify All Leads
  3. OutreachPro checks every lead with an email address (up to 100 at once)
  4. Results appear in the lead list: ✓ valid, ⚠ medium risk, ✗ invalid
  5. Filter to remove high-risk leads before sending any campaign

Before Every Campaign Send

Best practice: run verification before activating any new campaign. Go to Campaigns → [Campaign Name] → Verify Lead Emails. OutreachPro will flag how many leads in that campaign have risky emails, giving you the chance to remove them before they harm your sender score.

Why Bounce Rate Matters

Gmail and Outlook track bounce rates per sending domain. Above 2% bounces: deliverability warnings. Above 5%: your emails start going to spam for everyone — even people who want to receive them. One batch of unverified emails to 100 bad addresses can take weeks of warmup to recover from.

Finding Better Emails for Role-Based Addresses

When OutreachPro flags info@ or contact@ as medium-risk, use the Apify scraper to search for the specific owner: search Google Maps for the business name, then check their website's team/about page for a personal email. Direct emails to owners (max@fahrrad-mueller.de) convert at 5–10× the rate of generic addresses.

API

POST /api/verify/email     → {email} → {valid, risk, score, reason, hasMx}
POST /api/verify/bulk      → {emails[]} → {summary, results[]}
POST /api/verify/leads     → verify all leads in DB, update emailRisk field
