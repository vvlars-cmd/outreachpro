Smart Conditional Sequences

Standard sequences send the same follow-up to everyone on a fixed timer. Smart Sequences are different — they branch based on what each lead actually does. Opened your email? They go down path A. Didn't open? Path B. Replied? Sequence converts and stops. Every lead gets a personalised journey, not a mass cadence.

Why This Matters

Skylead charges $100/month just for conditional sequences. In OutreachPro, they're built in. A lead who opened your email three times is much hotter than one who never opened it — but without conditional sequences, you'd send them the same follow-up. Smart Sequences fix that.

How to Build a Smart Sequence

  1. Go to Sequences → Smart Sequences → New
  2. Add your first step (Day 0 — initial email)
  3. Click Add Condition on the step
  4. Set: If opened → go to Step 2A / If not opened → go to Step 2B
  5. Build Step 2A (for openers): shorter, more direct — they showed interest
  6. Build Step 2B (for non-openers): different subject line, different angle
  7. Click Enroll Leads to start the sequence

Example: CycleWASH 5-Step Smart Sequence

Step 1 (Day 0): Initial email — "Ihr Fahrradladen in {{city}}"
  ↓ IF opened →
    Step 2A (Day 3): "Sie haben meine E-Mail gelesen — darf ich fragen warum nicht?"
  ↓ IF not opened →
    Step 2B (Day 4): Completely different subject: "Kurze Frage zu {{name}}"
      ↓ IF 2B opened →
        Step 3A (Day 7): Product-focused follow-up
      ↓ IF 2B not opened →
        Step 3B (Day 10): Final attempt — breakup email

Step 4 (any path): LinkedIn connection request
  ↓ IF replied at any point → STOP — lead enters CRM as Replied

Condition Types

  • opened — lead opened the email (tracked via pixel)
  • replied — lead replied (sequence stops, CRM updated)
  • clicked — lead clicked a link in the email
  • not_opened — lead didn't open within the delay window

Real-World Performance

Conditional sequences consistently generate 2–3× more replies than flat sequences because:

  • Engaged leads (opened, clicked) get more direct messaging — not a generic follow-up
  • Unengaged leads get a completely different angle — new subject line, new hook
  • You never waste a follow-up step on someone who already replied

API

GET  /api/smart-sequences                     → list all
POST /api/smart-sequences                     → create sequence
POST /api/smart-sequences/:id/enroll          → enroll leads
POST /api/smart-sequences/:id/event           → record event {leadId, stepId, event}
GET  /api/smart-sequences/:id/enrollments     → list enrolled leads
